UX 1400 Reflection

Throughout this course, I have learned a lot about how users interact with products and how I should design these products with my end users in mind. Before this course, I had an interest in art and design, but focused purely on aesthetics. This course taught me how I can integrate usability into my designs to create products that are visually appealing, but can also be used efficiently and effectively by users.

I will admit, before taking this course, I did not see much value in a user experience education. I have always been interested in art as a hobby, but have never really thought that design would be a useful skill to have in the engineering industry. However, now that I have a better understanding of what user experience designers do, I could not imagine my career being possible without them. I now realize that behind every piece of software, every website, and every tool that I use, someone had to think about how people would interact with the object and come up with a way to make the experience as smooth as possible. Not only will user experience professionals enable me to do my job as an engineer, but they will also be necessary to ensure that the projects I work on are able to be used in the way I intend by customers.

User experience relates more to my major than I initially thought. As a mechanical engineering and computer science student, I will likely be working on products or designs that will be used by customers such as mechanical parts or software applications. Therefore, a background in user experience can be a very valuable tool for someone like me, so that when I'm designing, I can keep the client in mind and think from their point of view to provide them with the best possible experience.
