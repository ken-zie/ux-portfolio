Wireframing Project

Project Summary

Task

The purpose of this project was to conceptualize designs for the layout of our online portfolios. The goal of this project was to determine structure, layout, content, and functionality. We were required to hand-draw 8 wireframes and create 4 digital wireframes. Four of the hand-drawn wireframes and 2 of the digital wireframes were to be of the homepage, and the others were to be of a project page. The wireframes were to be completed individually.

Deliverable

The final product was 8 hand-drawn wireframes and 4 digital wireframes. Four of the hand-drawn wireframes and 2 of the digital wireframes were to be of the homepage, and the others were to be of a project page.

Timeframe

I had 1 week to create the 8 hand-drawn wireframes and 4 digital wireframes.

Constraints

The constraints of this project were that 8 hand-drawn wireframes were to be created first, and then 4 digital wireframes were to be created based off of what we liked about the hand-drawn wireframes.

Challenges

My main challenge with the wireframes was having to think about the most logical way to display information without any styling. In the past, whenever I have created a design, I first thought about how I wanted it to look and thought about layout after the fact. These wireframes forced me to think critically about how the user will gather information from the site. This way of thinking was very different for me, which presented a lot of challenges, but proved to be useful.

Project Analysis

Drawn

Digital

Successes

One aspect of the wireframes that turned out well was the hierarchy and emphasis of text. When creating the wireframes, I tried to think about how to differentiate the important text from the rest since that was a problem I struggled with during the poster project. In the wireframes, I decided to show the important text by making it larger. Not only did this help with information hierarchy, but it also helped add variation in the design, making it more appealing and interesting to look at.

Skills

One skill I improved upon throughout the creation of the wireframes was critical thinking about how a user interacts with a site if the styling has been removed. As I said in the "Poster Summary" section, whenever designing, I always start out with a base design and from there, add my information. I very rarely have thought about where to put the information put before having a style or an aesthetic set in place. When creating the wireframes, I had to think about what I wanted the user to see first, what I wanted them to think was most important, and how I wanted to present them with the information. Therefore, when placing components in the wireframes, I thought about how to make the most important text stand out, which pictures should be seen first and how they should relate to the text, and where to put the navigation so that the users are able to navigate my site with ease.

Improvement

Even though I improved in critical thinking about how users interact with my site, it is still an area that I can further improve on. In the future, if I were to continue this project, I would make a list of all components I want to appear on the page, including headings, text, and pictures. From there, I would rank them in order of importance and group them together in a logical way. I would use these lists and groupings then to help inform myself as to how I should place the components on the screen. However, I would probably do this after the drawn wireframes so that I still have some brainstormed ideas of designs.
