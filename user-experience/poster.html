Poster Project

Project Summary

Task

To create a poster promoting something of my choosing. Examples provided inlcuded a movie poster, event poster, or a location poster. The poster was to be completed individually, but peer and professor reviews were provided at various times throughout the duration of the project.

Deliverable

The final product was a complete poster that had incorporated peer and professor reviews, along with a written report on the project as a whole. This written report contained my thoughts on how well I did with the poster, what I feel still needs improvement, what changes I made based on feedback, and the steps I would take next if the project were to continue.

Timeframe

Project lasted three weeks. The project was split up among these three weeks, with a small portion of the project due at the end of each week.

Week 1: Poster Rough Draft - Created a rough draft of the poster and my professor provided critiques
Week 2: Poster v2 for Peer Reviews - Implemented changes to rough draft based on professor reviews so that peers could provide additional feedback
Week 3: Final Poster - Implemented changes to Poster v2 based on peer feedback

Constraints

The bare minimum requirements for the poster were a title, all necessary information, and at least one picture.

Challenges

My main challenge with this project was trying to find a way to layout all of the information in a logical and simple way. I found this to be challenging because I have a tendancy toward a more "cluttered" aesthetic, rather than clean and simple.

Final

Successes

One aspect of the poster that turned out well was the overall theme. The NASA RMC Lunabotics organization is engineering focused, and I wanted to portray that in the poster. I did so by using a picture of a NASA Mars rover for the background, chose a simple and straightforward font, and made the separation lines resemble circuitry.

Skills

One skill I improved upon throuhgout the creation of this poster was information hierarchy. My poster contains a lot of information because the organization has a general team meeting and three subteam meetings, all of which have different meeting times. In my rough draft, it took a lot of time to pick out what the most important information was. In the second version, I placed more emphasis on the full team meeting by making it larger and separating it from the subteam meetings. The second version was then reviewed by my peers, and each one said I might benefit from including an eye catching color. I then decided to place even more emphasis on the full team meeting by making it green so that if someone were to glance at the poster, thier eyes would first go to the large title, and then to the brightly colored full team meeting. I wanted to highlight the title and the full team meeting because they are most important.

Improvement

For my next project, I would like to improve upon simplicity within the design. When designing, I tend to make the designs busy because I like a more cluttered aesthetic. However, this makes it very hard for users to quickly and easily find important information. I noticed this when I was creating the poster because I had a tendency to include lots of unnecessary information, simply for aesthetic purposes. Therefore, for all my upcoming projets, I would like to improve by keeping the information simple and to the point.
