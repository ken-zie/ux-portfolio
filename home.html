Portfolio Design Rationale

This website was created for my Foundations of User Experience course, taken during my sophomore year at the Milwaukee School of Engineering. The purpose of creating this portfolio is the showcase my design skills to future potential employers. Therefore, while creating the webpage I had to think about how my primary audience would be human resource professionals or engineers who are looking to hire an engineer, and had to make design choices accordingly.

One choice I made about the website was to make clear distinctions between each section within the page. For example, on my homepage, I have clear distinctions between this section where I am discussing my design rationale, and the section below that has information about me. I made these distinctions by separating the content so that only one section displays on the screen at one time, and made the backgrounds of each section different. I did this so that the readers are not bombarded with information and can easily focus on one section at a time.

Another design decision I made was to bold and brighten the important text within sections. I understand that HR representatives and engineers have a lot of work to do, and maybe do not want to spend a long time reading paragraphs of information. By highlighting the key text, readers are able to skim the site and still get all necessary information, and if they want to learn more, they can read the non highlighted text. I also broke up the text on the project pages into sections with a heading for each so that the reader knows what information they will learn before reading the text.

A majority of the design choices made for this website were decided on so that potential employers could quickly gather as much information as they felt necessary, while also maintaining a simple and clean aesthetic design.
